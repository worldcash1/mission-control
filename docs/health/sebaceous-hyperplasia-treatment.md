# Sebaceous Hyperplasia Treatment Research

*Last updated: Jan 29, 2026*

## Overview

Sebaceous hyperplasia = enlarged oil glands causing small bumps on face. Benign but cosmetically annoying.

---

## Treatment Methods Comparison

### 1450nm Diode Laser (Smoothbeam) ‚≠ê BEST
- Targets water/oil in sebaceous gland, shrinks from inside
- Healing: 1-3 days (fastest)
- Sessions: 2-3 needed
- Recurrence: 10-15% (lowest)
- Scarring risk: Very low
- Cost: $300-400/session (US)

### CO2 Laser
- Vaporizes tissue layer by layer from surface
- Healing: 5-7 days
- Sessions: Usually 1
- Recurrence: 15-20%
- Scarring risk: Low (very precise)
- Cost: $300-500/session (US), $1000-3500/bump (HK)

### Electrocautery / ThermoCare
- Electric current burns/destroys tissue from surface
- Healing: 7-14 days (slowest)
- Sessions: Usually 1
- Recurrence: 20-30% (highest)
- Scarring risk: Moderate
- Cost: $100-200/session (US), $200-400/bump (HK)
